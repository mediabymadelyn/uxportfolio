DoodleQuest

An AI-assisted creative prompting system designed to help artists build skills, overcome creative blocks, and explore identity through personalized, ethical guidance.

Back to Work

Overview

DoodleQuest is an AI-assisted creative coaching concept for beginner and intermediate artists. The goal is to support growth through prompts that are adaptive, inclusive, and open-ended rather than rigid or prescriptive.

The project started as a basic Java prompt generator and evolved into an interdisciplinary research and design initiative connected to my independent study and CS + Studio Art track.

Problem

  • Generic prompts do not adapt to goals, skill levels, or personal context.
  • Many AI prompts default to Eurocentric references and narrow visual norms.
  • Literal prompts can flatten creativity and feel assignment-like.
  • Most systems treat each prompt as one-off, with no long-term memory.
  • Identity, emotion, and cultural nuance are often ignored in prompt generation.

My Role

  • Conducted autoethnographic independent-study research over one semester.
  • Created traditional and digital artworks to evaluate prompt quality and outcomes.
  • Developed a prompt evaluation rubric: clarity, inclusivity, creativity, flexibility, and engagement.
  • Identified model limitations around bias, memory, and cultural assumptions.
  • Defined user needs and interaction priorities for future product development.

Research Foundations

AI Reinforces Dominant Norms

Prompt tests frequently surfaced Western symbolism and narrow aesthetic assumptions, which can reduce cultural relevance and expressive range.

Prompt Quality Is Contextual

Strong prompts differ by skill level, emotional state, and personal background; there is no universal best prompt structure.

Autonomy Improves Creative Outcomes

Open-ended prompts consistently generated more meaningful, personal work than literal instructions.

Prompting Is a Learnable Skill

Artists benefit when they can guide AI intentionally instead of accepting default suggestions.

Long-Term Memory Matters

Growth-oriented creative coaching requires continuity across sessions, not isolated interactions.

Design Goals

Prompting Modes

  • Art Block Mode: exploratory prompts based on interests and constraints.
  • Skill-Building Mode: targeted practice for gesture, composition, shading, and color.
  • Identity Mode: prompts grounded in personal, cultural, and emotional themes.

Memory-Aware Interaction

  • Tracks skill goals, preferred themes, and prior outputs.
  • Evolves prompt complexity over time as users improve.
  • Supports a coaching-style relationship instead of random output.

Ethical & Inclusive UX

  • Avoids defaulting to narrow cultural assumptions.
  • Encourages interpretation, experimentation, and personal voice.
  • Frames AI as a collaborator, not an authority.

Future Development

  • User accounts with personalized creative profiles.
  • A conversational DoodleBot with reflection prompts.
  • Challenge modes and skill-practice modules.
  • Long-term memory and progress tracking.
  • Feedback loops tied to user reflections and artwork uploads.

Reflection

DoodleQuest reinforced that AI creativity tools should behave like thoughtful collaborators. Prompt design is not neutral, and strong systems must support autonomy, cultural nuance, and long-term growth, not just quick output generation.
