About

I’m a designer and builder focused on digital tools for creative and learning-focused work.

I study computer science and studio art at Occidental, and I work across research, design, and code to build tools people actually use.

Who I Am

I’m Madelyn (Maddi), and I’m interested in building learning tools, creative systems, and research interfaces.

I work across product design and frontend code. I talk to users, sketch ideas, prototype quickly, and build things end to end. Studying both CS and studio art means I approach problems from both a technical and visual perspective.

Background

Computer science shapes how I think about systems. Art shapes how I think about visuals and communication. I rely on both in my work.

Right Now

I’m building ItEra Studio as my senior project, an AI search tool that helps artists find real reference images quickly and easily.

How I Work

How I work

Start with listening

I talk to people, understand what they actually need, sketch flows, prioritize features, and decide what matters most.

Build and refine

I prototype in Figma and code, test ideas, and refine based on what I learn.

I studied abroad in Tokyo, which made me more aware of how design decisions land differently across cultures.

Beyond Work

Outside of work

I run, lift, draw, paint, play games (The Sims 4 is undefeated), watch video essays, and stay active in the Black Student Alliance and CS Club. These experiences shape how I think about people and design.
