About

A designer-technologist grounded in code, craft, and human-centered thinking.

I build at the intersection of computer science, studio art, and HCI, with a focus on products that make learning and creativity more intuitive.

Who I Am

Hi, I am Madelyn (Maddi), a designer-technologist building thoughtful digital experiences.

I work across product design and frontend implementation, with a strong focus on learning tools, creative systems, and interfaces that make complex ideas feel intuitive. My approach combines design research, interaction craft, and technical execution so concepts can move from strategy to shipped interface.

Academic Foundation

I study Computer Science and Studio Art at Occidental College, which gives me a hybrid lens: systems thinking from engineering and visual storytelling from art practice.

Current Focus

I am developing DoodleQuest as my senior project, translating independent research on AI prompting and creativity into an interactive product with real learning outcomes.

How I Work

A process grounded in research, iteration, and cross-functional thinking.

Research to Product Direction

I start by clarifying user goals, pain points, and context. Then I map product opportunities, prioritize flows, and define the decisions that will have the biggest impact.

Interface Craft + Implementation

I prototype quickly in Figma and code, test interaction assumptions early, and refine the visual system so the final experience feels both usable and intentional.

A semester studying abroad in Tokyo expanded my perspective on accessibility, communication styles, and audience context. It strengthened the way I design for diverse users and collaborate across different disciplines.

Beyond Work

What keeps my creativity sharp outside design projects.

I recharge through running, strength training, drawing and painting, gaming (especially The Sims 4), fashion, and long-form video essays. On campus, I am active in the Black Student Alliance and the Computer Science Club. These communities and practices continue to shape my perspective as both a builder and collaborator.
